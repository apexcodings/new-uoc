To help answer specific questions about the safety and effectiveness of certain drugs, vaccines, and therapies, or to explore new ways of using existing treatments, doctors and other healthcare professionals often perform Clinical Trials. These voluntary research studies are performed according to strict guidelines set by the Food and Drug Administration to ensure volunteers are treated as safely as possible.

Before being accepted for participation in a clinical trial, all volunteers must quality for the study by specific inclusion and exclusion criteria.

If you’re interested in volunteering for clinical trials at University Orthopedics Center, you can find out more information by visiting clinical trials.gov and fda.gov/oashi/clinicaltrials. You can also visit trials@uoc.com to learn more about specific trials being conducted at our facilities.

To speak with someone about participation in the clinical trials being conducted at University Orthopedics Center, please contact our Trials department at trials@uoc.com or call 814-942-1166 if you have any questions.

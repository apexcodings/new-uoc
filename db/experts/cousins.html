Background

Dr. Cousins is a fellowship trained interventional pain management physician. He joined University Orthopedics Center (UOC) in July of 2003 and sees patients at our Geisinger Gray’s Woods office located in Port Matilda.

Dr. Cousins completed his Pain Management Fellowship at Temple University School of Medicine at the Western Pennsylvania Hospital Campus in 2003. His training and experience include unique competencies in the treatment of arthritis, back and neck pain, nerve pain, and shingles.

After receiving his medical degree from the Philadelphia College of Osteopathic Medicine in May 1988, Dr. Cousins completed a rotating internship at Millcreek Community Hospital and an anesthesiology residency at The Western Pennsylvania Hospital.

Prior to joining UOC, Dr. Cousins was a staff anesthesiologist at Bradford Regional Medical Center from 1993-1997, where he became the chairman of Department of Anesthesia in 1994. From 1997 to 2002 Dr. Cousins practiced medicine at Centre Community Surgical Center. During that time he became the Department of Anesthesia chairman at Tyrone Hospital, Tyrone, PA.

Dr. Cousins is board certified from the American Board of Anesthesiology and the American Board of Pain Medicine. He is a member of the American Society of Anesthesiologists, the International Spinal Injection Society, the American Society of Interventional Pain Physicians, and the Pennsylvania Society of Anesthesiologists.

Dr. Cousins lives with his wife and children in Lemont, PA. He enjoys running and coaching his children’s sports activities.

Dr. Cousins is currently accepting new patients at our Geisinger Gray’s Woods office located in Port Matilda. For an appointment please call (814) 272-5011.
